Services

We offer a wide variety of services to our patients, check how we can best help you below.

Annual Physical/Wellness Exam

Complete head-to-toe exam, optionally including vision screening, vital signs, review of chronic health issues, yearly lab work, and age-related screenings (mammograms, colonoscopies, etc.). Includes:
* Sports and school physicals
* Medicare wellness exams
* Regular annual physicals

Chronic Condition Management

Evaluate effectiveness of current treatment and adjust medication as needed. Routine follow-ups for chronic health issues including:
* Hypertension
* Type II diabetes
* Hyperlipidemia
* Hypothyroidism
* Allergies, reflux, and more...

Acute Sick Visit

In-house point-of-care testing to make a diagnosis and prescribing effective treatment. May require external testing (lab work, imaging, etc.). Management of acute illnesses or issues, including:
* Cough
* Ear Pain
* Sore throat
* UTI
* Rashes, and more...

Weight Management

Includes initial consultation, evaluating goals, reviewing medical history, obtaining labs if needed, starting a weight loss plan based on each individual's needs. We offer the best local pricing on GLP-1 (semaglutide) injections for those unable to obtain through insurance.

Anxiety & Depression

We manage general anxiety and general depression through routine follow-ups and medication evaluation. We will help outline the treatment regimen that best helps you, optionally including in-house testing through GeneSight to confirm which medications work best based on your DNA. Specialist referrals are available if extra assistance is needed.

STI Diagnosis & Treatment

We can obtain in-house urine and swabs to test for sexually transmitted diseases based on known exposure or presenting symptoms. Blood work is optional. Our tests include:
* Gonhorrea
* Chlamydia
* HIV
* Hepatitis A/B/C
* Thrichomoniasis, and more...

Frequently Asked Questions

Please check below before scheduling an appointment to make your experience as smooth as possible.

General Information

Is the clinic handicap-accessible?

Yes! Feel free to call in advance if you have any special requirements and we will do our best to honor them.

Do you accept walk-ins, or is it appointment only?

We do not accept walk-ins, but same-day appointments may be available. Please call our office to check if we have any same-day appointments open.

Appointments and Scheduling

How do I schedule an appointment?

Please call (270) 885-9995 during our normal working hours. Be prepared with the following information:
* Reason for your visit (check our Services tab)
* Insurance information (check our Insurances Covered tab)
* Card/payment information
* Current address

What should I bring to my appointment?

Please bring the following:
* Valid driver's license or other government-issued ID
* Payment method of choice
* Insurance card
* All current medications

What is your cancellation/missed appointment policy?

Our team's time is valuable, so appointments cancelled without enough time for us to schedule another patient must be compensated. Our fees are billed as below:
* Cancellation 24 business hours or more before scheduled appointment time = no fee
* Cancellation less than 24 business hours before scheduled appointment = $25 cancellation fee
* Missed appointment with no call = $25 no-show fee
* Arriving 15+ minutes late = provider's discretion, but typically appointment is cancelled + $25 no-show fee
Three or more missed appointments within 12 months is grounds for termination of services.

Do you offer telehealth appointments?

No, not currently. We may offer telehealth opportunities in the future.

Billing and Payments

What payment types do you accept?

We accept cash, check, and card.

Do you offer services for uninsured patients?

Yes, we offer a flat rate of $100 (subject to change) for self-pay patients. Additional charges will apply if lab work is required.

Patient Care

Do you see pediatric patients?

We only see patients ages 5 and up.

How do you handle prescription refills?

It is the patient's responsiblity to notify us of your refill request with enough time (3 business days) for us to process it. Typically, the pharmacy will send us a refill request when you pick up your last refill. We normally go over any necessary refills during your follow-up visit. If you need a refill between visits, please contact us.

Do you offer vaccinations and immunizations?

We only offer the flu vaccine during flu season (September through March). We recommend going to your preferred pharmacy or health department to stay up-to-date on your vaccines.

What if I need a specialist?

Most specialists require a referral from your primary care provider, so please schedule an appointment with us so we can discuss your needs and what referral needs to be made.

Emergency and Contact

I have an urgent issue/emergency! Who do I call?

If you have life-threatening symptoms (chest pain, shortness of breath, symptoms of stroke, excessive bleeding, etc.) CALL 911 IMMEDIATELY.
If you are an established patient and urgently need to contact our office after-hours, please use the on-call number: (270) 498-3932 (call or text)
If you urgently need to call our office during working hours, use the front desk number: (270) 885-9995

How can I get in touch with Sandra Brown directly?

If you are an existing patient, you can send Sandra Brown a message through your AthenaHealth portal. Any other forms of contact (Facebook, personal cell, etc.) are not HIPAA compliant and will not receive a response.
If you are a new patient, another member of our team will gladly answer your questions or convey your concerns to Sandra Brown as needed.
